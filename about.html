Let’s take it way back. Ironically, my original interest in design stemmed from the absence of it in my childhood home. I’m not just talking about the floral couches and colored carpets, but the overall layout and use of space left something to be desired as well. I was far from an interior designer yet I found myself pondering ways to improve my home. Now that I recall, what was I really pondering? I can define it now: how could I elevate the user experience of my childhood house?


As a young, developing artist: conceptualization was my craft. Type of medium, every shot, word, transition, etc. – all decisions needed to lend itself to the concept. How? Why? Who? Always considered for every choice. At some point in my career as a visual designer the hows, whys, and whos started to fade away. Embracing the UX design process brought those questions back to the forefront.


Present day, I am committed to staying connected to the core concept, problem, pain point, all of the above and/or whatever it may be. Inquisition and iteration are mandatory. My visual chops and conceptual artistry are finally aligned: I am a product designer with a big nose!
